## Why this playbook exists

During incidents, teams lose time when ownership and evidence standards are unclear.

This playbook keeps Claude-assisted recovery deterministic under pressure.

## Incident severity matrix

| Level | Typical impact | Default posture |
|---|---|---|
| SEV-1 | auth/billing/data correctness at risk | immediate rollback candidate |
| SEV-2 | major workflow degraded | feature-flag or targeted hotfix |
| SEV-3 | non-critical behavior regression | scheduled corrective patch |

## First-15-minutes protocol

- declare severity and incident owner
- freeze unrelated merges on impacted surface
- capture first evidence snapshot (logs, failing checks, scope)
- assign next owner per lane

No owner + no snapshot means no reliable control.

## Recovery lane split

Run lanes in parallel with explicit boundaries:

- **Investigation lane**: root-cause hypothesis and reproduction
- **Mitigation lane**: rollback/hotfix preparation
- **Communication lane**: status timeline and stakeholder updates
- **Verification lane**: regression test + rollback safety checks

Lane overlap without ownership usually creates duplicate work and blind spots.

## Decision ladder

1. Feature-flag disable (fastest reversible option)
2. Targeted hotfix (when scope is narrow and testable)
3. Full rollback (for high uncertainty or safety-critical surfaces)

Record why options were rejected, not only why one was chosen.

## Evidence packet template

Every decision checkpoint should include:

- current severity + owner
- impacted scope
- command/log evidence
- residual risks
- next action + deadline

## Post-incident retro minimum

Within 24 hours, write:

- trigger and detection gap
- what slowed mitigation
- what made recovery faster
- guardrail change proposal
- owner + due date

Incidents are expensive lessons. Capture them while details are fresh.

## Advanced anti-patterns

### One-lane hero mode

Fast at first, fragile later. Split lanes early.

### Hotfix merged without timeline communication

You ship code, but teams still operate on stale assumptions.

### Incident closed before fresh verification rerun

A green statement without rerun evidence is not closure.

## Quick checklist

Before closing:

- severity + owner documented
- mitigation decision recorded
- verification rerun evidence attached
- follow-up guardrail task assigned

Claude helps with speed. This playbook preserves control while moving fast.
